What is Sn?

The symmetric group Sn is the group of all permutations of n elements, with function composition as the group operation. It has n! elements.

Cycle Notation

A permutation can be written as a product of disjoint cycles. For example, (1 2 3) means 1 → 2 → 3 → 1. The notation (1 2 3)(4 5) represents a permutation where 1, 2, 3 cycle among themselves and 4, 5 swap.

Properties

Order: The smallest positive integer k such that σk = identity. It equals the LCM of the cycle lengths.

Sign: A permutation is even (+1) if it can be written as a product of an even number of transpositions, and odd (-1) otherwise. The sign equals (-1)n - c where c is the number of cycles (including fixed points).

Key Facts

• |Sn| = n!
• Sn is non-abelian for n ≥ 3

• Every permutation is a product of transpositions
• The alternating group An consists of all even permutations
• |An| = n!/2 for n ≥ 2
